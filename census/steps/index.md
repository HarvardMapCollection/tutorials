# Working with census data for digital mapping

This tutorial series will equip you with the skills needed to use United States census data to create a digital map.

## Lessons

[Lesson 01 - Getting the software](https://harvardmapcollection.github.io/tutorials/qgis/download/)
You will need a desktop software GIS software to perform the necessary data edits to the census data. In this tutorial we will use the free software, QGIS. 

[Lesson 02 - Getting the data](https://harvardmapcollection.github.io/tutorials/census/obtaining-census-data/)
To get usable files for mapping, you must download two files: (1) a table with statistics and (2) a GIS boundary file. These files can be obtained from a few sources.

[Lesson 03 - Joining the data](https://harvardmapcollection.github.io/tutorials/qgis/join)
To create usable mapping files, you will need to know how to join mapping shape data to statistical files.

[Lesson 04 - Filtering or clipping the data](https://harvardmapcollection.github.io/tutorials/qgis/clip)
To account for the large size of census data files, and to have more control over the look of your map, it's helpful to know how to filter and clip datasets to focused areas of interest.


[Lesson 05 - Normalizing the data](https://harvardmapcollection.github.io/tutorials/qgis/normalize)
Census data is often made available as raw counts, rather than ratios or proportions. This makes gleaning meaningful insights from the data on a map difficult. This lesson covers how to convert raw counts to percentages. 

[Lesson 06 - Styling the data](https://harvardmapcollection.github.io/tutorials/census/steps/style)
Now that you have reliable, usable map data, you can use it to create a map.
